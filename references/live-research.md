# Live Research

Use this file whenever the task needs current guidance rather than frozen memory.

## When Live Research Is Mandatory

Browse current sources when:
- the user asks for research, latest guidance, or verification
- the task involves shipping or readiness claims
- store listing behavior or icon testing matters
- platform rules may have changed
- premium/perception claims need evidence
- the brief depends on current competitor patterns

## Current Notes To Verify Live

Do not treat these as permanent truths without checking the live pages when they matter.

As of April 7, 2026:
- Android Developers states that starting with Android 16 QPR 2, Android automatically themes app icons for apps that do not provide their own themed treatment, raising the importance of a deliberate monochrome icon path.
- Apple Product Page Optimization supports testing alternate app icons, screenshots, and previews, and Apple recommends waiting until a treatment is declared better or worse than baseline with at least 90% confidence before applying it.
- Apple App Review Guidelines include a specific rule that you cannot use another developer's icon, brand, or product name in your app's icon or name without approval.

## Research Interpretation Rules

- Do not use a research finding as a blanket style rule.
- Use recent findings to sharpen hypotheses, not to replace project fit.
- When a study and the product context disagree, explain the tradeoff instead of pretending there is one universal answer.
- Prefer official platform rules for shipping constraints and peer-reviewed studies for perceptual claims.
