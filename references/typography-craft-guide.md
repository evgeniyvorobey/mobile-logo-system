# Typography Craft — Full Guide

This is the full educational reference for typography. The runtime checklist is in `typography-craft.md`.

## Type Classification For Logo Work

### Geometric Sans
Construction: circles, squares, uniform strokes. Character: modern, clean, rational.
Examples: Futura, Avenir, Montserrat, Product Sans.
Best with: geometric symbols, clean silhouettes. Risk: cold, generic.

### Humanist Sans
Construction: calligraphy-inspired modulation, no serifs. Character: warm, approachable.
Examples: Gill Sans, Frutiger, Myriad, Source Sans.
Best with: organic symbols, health/lifestyle. Risk: too soft for premium/tech.

### Neo-Grotesque
Construction: uniform weight, closed apertures. Character: neutral, serious.
Examples: Helvetica, Arial, Univers, Aktiv Grotesk.
Best with: confident symbols that carry personality. Risk: adds nothing on its own.

### Slab Serif
Construction: uniform-thickness serifs, geometric base. Character: sturdy, editorial.
Examples: Rockwell, Clarendon, Roboto Slab, Zilla Slab.
Best with: bold symbols, heritage brands. Risk: heavy at small sizes, serifs clog at 29px.

### Modern Serif (Didone)
Construction: extreme thick-thin contrast, hairline serifs. Character: elegant, luxury.
Examples: Didot, Bodoni, Playfair Display.
Best with: luxury/fashion/editorial. Risk: hairlines disappear below ~14px.

### Display / Custom
Bespoke lettering for maximum differentiation. Risk: expensive, hard to maintain.

## Symbol-Type Pairing — Details

### Contrast Pairing
Symbol and wordmark from different families. Example: geometric symbol + humanist sans.
Creates visual interest, each element has a clear role. Use when symbol has strong personality.

### Harmony Pairing
Symbol and wordmark share structural logic. Example: geometric symbol + geometric sans with same stroke widths.
Creates unified system. Use when brand is about consistency and precision.

### Weight Matching
Visual weight (stroke thickness, counter size, density) must match between symbol and wordmark. Test: squint at both side by side — should feel like they belong together.

### Size Relationship
Common ratios (wordmark cap-height to symbol height):
- 100%: equal partnership
- 60-70%: symbol-dominant (most common for app logos)
- 40-50%: symbol is primary, wordmark subordinate

## Optical Spacing — Details

### Kerning Principles
Default kerning is for body text, not logo scale. Evaluate every pair:
1. round-round (OO, CO): tighten slightly
2. straight-straight (HI, MN): standard
3. round-straight (OI, DO): between the two
4. open pairs (LA, LT, VA, WA, TA): tighten significantly
5. problem pairs (rn vs m, cl vs d): ensure no merge at small size

### Tracking
- tight: confident, premium, risky at small sizes
- standard: safe, readable
- loose: luxury/editorial, can feel scattered at small sizes

For icon lockups: slightly loose to prevent letter merging.
For external branding: tighter usually appropriate.

### Baseline Alignment
Don't align baselines mathematically. Align visual center of wordmark to visual center of symbol. If descenders exist, raise baseline to compensate.

## Lockup Construction — Details

### Horizontal
Symbol left, wordmark right (LTR). Gap: 50-100% of cap-height.

### Stacked
Symbol above, wordmark below, centered. Gap: 30-60% of cap-height. Wordmark width should not exceed symbol width by >30%.

### Icon-Only
No wordmark. For app icons, favicons, notifications.

### Wordmark-Only
No symbol. For splash screens, marketing headers, name-first brands.

### Clear Space
Cap-height of wordmark on all four sides. Nothing enters the clear space zone.

## Case Strategy

- **all lowercase**: friendly, modern, casual. Risk: unserious for finance/enterprise.
- **Title Case**: balanced, professional, safe default.
- **ALL UPPERCASE**: bold, authoritative. Risk: aggressive, harder to scan at small sizes.
- **Custom Case** (camelCase): tech, developer tools.

## Wordmark Customization

### Safe Customizations
- adjusting a single terminal
- creating a ligature
- modifying the dot on 'i' or 'j' to echo the symbol
- adjusting descender/ascender for lockup fit

### Dangerous Customizations
- changing fundamental letter proportions
- removing recognizable features (crossbar from 'A')
- decorative elements that fail at small size

Rule: customization visible at medium size, invisible at small size.
